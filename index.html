Why It Matters

Forests cover nearly 31% of the planet's land area and provide essential services — absorbing carbon dioxide, producing oxygen, and maintaining biodiversity. Yet, every year, about 10 million hectares of forest are lost due to human activity.

This loss contributes to 20% of global carbon emissions, worsens climate change, and disrupts the lives of indigenous communities who depend on forests for food and culture.

Causes of Deforestation

🌾 Agricultural Expansion

Forests are cleared to create land for crops and livestock.

Agriculture causes around 80% of global deforestation. Palm oil, soy, and cattle ranching dominate this issue. When forest land becomes farmland, biodiversity drops and soil quality declines, increasing erosion and flooding risks.

🪵 Logging

Cutting trees for timber, paper, and fuelwood.

Illegal and unsustainable logging contributes to habitat loss and greenhouse gas emissions. Many tropical countries suffer from poor enforcement, allowing companies to harvest wood faster than trees can regrow.

🏗️ Urban and Infrastructure Development

As cities expand, forests are replaced by roads and buildings.

Urbanization fragments ecosystems, increases surface temperatures, and reduces natural water absorption, leading to floods and poor air quality.

Effects of Deforestation

🌡️ Climate Change

Trees absorb CO₂; losing them speeds global warming.

Forests act as carbon sinks. When cut or burned, they release stored carbon, contributing to rising global temperatures and extreme weather patterns.

🐘 Loss of Biodiversity

Thousands of species lose their habitats every year.

Over 80% of terrestrial animals live in forests. Deforestation threatens species like orangutans, tigers, and elephants, pushing many toward extinction.

💧 Water Cycle Disruption

Trees regulate rainfall and maintain groundwater levels.

When trees vanish, soil dries faster, rivers shrink, and rainfall patterns change — increasing droughts and floods.

👩‍🌾 Human Impact

Communities lose homes, food sources, and livelihoods.

Indigenous people depend on forests for survival. Deforestation displaces them, destroys traditions, and increases poverty in rural areas.
