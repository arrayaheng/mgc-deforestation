üå± Purpose of This Project

This website was created to raise awareness about deforestation ‚Äî one of the most urgent environmental problems facing our planet. The goal is to educate others about what causes deforestation, its devastating effects, and the actions we can take to stop it.

Forests are essential for life: they purify air, stabilize the climate, and support more than 80% of all land-based species. Yet every minute, we lose over 10 million hectares of forest (FAO, 2023). This project serves as both a call to action and a guide to understanding how our choices impact the Earth.

üî• Causes of Deforestation

Agricultural Expansion

The leading cause of deforestation worldwide, accounting for nearly 80% of forest loss (WWF, 2024). Land is cleared for crops like soybeans, palm oil, and cattle ranching. As demand for food and biofuels increases, so does pressure on tropical forests.

Logging and Timber Industry

Logging removes millions of hectares of forest each year for timber, paper, and furniture (FAO, 2023). Illegal operations and lack of replanting worsen the destruction and disrupt biodiversity.

Mining and Industrial Development

Mining for minerals, oil, and gas destroys ecosystems and contaminates soil and rivers (UNEP, 2023). Deforestation for industrial zones also displaces local and Indigenous communities.

Urbanization and Infrastructure

Roads, housing, and cities expand rapidly into forested areas (World Bank, 2024). This fragments habitats and reduces green cover critical for climate regulation.

üåç Effects of Deforestation

Loss of Biodiversity

Forests are home to 80% of terrestrial species (UNEP, 2023). Their destruction causes mass extinction and ecological imbalance that affects humans, too.

Climate Change

Forests act as carbon sinks. Cutting them releases carbon dioxide, fueling global warming (NASA, 2024). This contributes to rising sea levels and extreme weather.

Water Cycle Disruption

Forests control rainfall and humidity. Removing them leads to droughts, floods, and unstable water supplies (Rainforest Alliance, 2024).

Soil Erosion and Desertification

Without trees, soil erodes and loses fertility (ScienceDirect, 2024). This leads to food insecurity and desert expansion.
