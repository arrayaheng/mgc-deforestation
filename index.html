üå± Purpose of This Project

This website was created to raise awareness about deforestation ‚Äî one of the most urgent environmental problems facing our planet. The goal is to educate others about what causes deforestation, its devastating effects, and the actions we can take to stop it.

Forests are essential for life: they purify air, stabilize the climate, and support more than 80% of all land-based species. Yet every minute, we lose over 10 million hectares of forest (FAO, 2023). This project serves as both a call to action and a guide to understanding how our choices impact the Earth.

üî• Causes of Deforestation

Agricultural Expansion

Agriculture drives 80% of global deforestation. Forests are cleared for crops like palm oil, soybeans, and livestock grazing. The demand for global food and biofuel has increased land conversion rates dramatically (WWF, 2024).

Logging Industry

Both legal and illegal logging contribute to habitat loss. The timber is used for furniture, paper, and construction materials. Lack of replanting after logging worsens biodiversity loss and increases soil erosion (FAO, 2023).

Mining Operations

Large-scale mining for gold, coal, and oil destroys ecosystems. It also leads to contamination of rivers and displacement of Indigenous people (UNEP, 2023).

Urbanization & Infrastructure

Expanding cities and roads fragment forests, reducing green cover and harming wildlife migration. Rapid urban development is one of the fastest-growing threats (World Bank, 2024).

üåç Effects of Deforestation

Loss of Biodiversity

80% of terrestrial animals live in forests. When trees disappear, species lose their homes and food sources, leading to extinction (UNEP, 2023).

Climate Change

Forests absorb CO‚ÇÇ; when they‚Äôre cut, that carbon is released into the atmosphere, accelerating global warming (NASA, 2024).

Water Cycle Disruption

Forests regulate rainfall and groundwater. Deforestation causes droughts and flooding, impacting both humans and wildlife (Rainforest Alliance, 2024).

Soil Erosion

Tree roots anchor soil. Without them, land erodes easily, destroying crops and turning fertile ground into desert (ScienceDirect, 2024).
