About This Project

This website raises awareness about deforestation ‚Äî a major global issue that threatens biodiversity, contributes to climate change, and impacts human lives. It was created to educate, inform, and inspire action.

Forests are the Earth's lungs. They absorb carbon dioxide, release oxygen, and provide habitats for millions of species. Understanding deforestation helps us protect ecosystems and ensure a sustainable future.

üî• Causes of Deforestation

Agricultural Expansion

The expansion of farmland for crops like soy, palm oil, and cattle grazing accounts for about 70‚Äì80% of global deforestation. As global food demand rises, forests are cleared to create new farmland, especially in tropical regions such as the Amazon Basin.

Logging and Timber Industry

Logging provides materials for construction, paper, and furniture. However, unsustainable and illegal logging operations destroy millions of hectares each year. The removal of old-growth trees affects carbon storage and species habitats.

Mining and Extraction

Mining for resources such as gold, coal, and rare earth minerals leads to extensive forest clearance. Toxic runoff contaminates rivers, displacing communities and damaging wildlife.

Urbanization

Expanding cities need more land for infrastructure, housing, and industries. Urbanization fragments ecosystems, isolates wildlife, and increases human impact on natural landscapes.

üåç Effects of Deforestation

Loss of Biodiversity

Over 80% of terrestrial species live in forests. When habitats are destroyed, thousands of species face extinction, upsetting food chains and weakening ecosystems that humans rely on.

Climate Change

Trees act as carbon sinks. Deforestation releases stored CO‚ÇÇ, accelerating global warming. It contributes to approximately 10% of global greenhouse gas emissions annually.

Water Cycle Disruption

Forests help regulate rainfall and maintain soil moisture. When removed, rainfall patterns change, leading to droughts, floods, and reduced agricultural productivity.

Soil Erosion

Tree roots hold soil together. When cleared, soil erosion increases, reducing land fertility and causing landslides in vulnerable areas.
