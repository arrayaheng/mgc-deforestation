Agricultural Expansion: Clearing forests for farms and livestock.
Farmers cut down trees to make space for crops or cattle. While it boosts food production, it destroys biodiversity and can permanently change the landscape.

Urban Growth: Expanding cities take over forest areas.

Mining: Extracting minerals clears vast forest areas.
Mining operations strip vegetation and pollute nearby rivers and soil with chemicals, leaving long-term damage.
Wildfires: Natural or human-caused fires destroy forests.
Large fires can wipe out forests within days, releasing carbon and harming air quality for miles.

üå± Solutions to Deforestation

Reforestation: Planting trees restores balance.
Replanting trees helps rebuild ecosystems, improve air quality, and store carbon dioxide again.
Sustainable Farming: Using eco-friendly agricultural practices.
Crop rotation and agroforestry reduce the need for land clearing, protecting natural forests.
Protected Areas: Creating reserves to preserve forests.
Governments can set aside conservation zones that prevent illegal logging and mining.
Eco-Friendly Products: Buy certified sustainable goods.
Choosing products with eco-labels (like FSC wood) discourages illegal deforestation and waste.
Education & Awareness: Teaching communities to protect forests.
Awareness campaigns and education help people understand the value of forests and how to conserve them.
