Welcome

Forests cover about 31% of Earth’s land, but we are losing 10 million hectares every year. That’s equal to 27 football fields every minute. Forests are essential for clean air, water, biodiversity, and climate stability.

Causes of Deforestation

Click to expand details:

Huge areas are cleared for palm oil, soybeans, and cattle ranching. Example: Amazon rainforest loses vast land to cattle farms.

Commercial and illegal logging remove trees faster than they regrow.

Expanding cities, highways, and dams replace forests.

Forest fires, natural and man-made, destroy habitats rapidly.

Environmental Problems

Forests store 289 billion tons of carbon. Deforestation releases CO₂, worsening global warming.

80% of land animals live in forests. Orangutans, tigers, and jaguars are losing their homes.

Forests regulate rainfall and water storage. Without them, droughts and floods rise.

Tree roots stabilize soil. Without forests, fertile soil washes away.

Impacts on People

Over 1.6 billion people depend on forests for food, medicine, or income. Deforestation causes:

  • Loss of indigenous lands and cultures.
  • Reduced clean water sources for nearby communities.
  • Increased spread of zoonotic diseases like Ebola and COVID-19.

Solutions

Planting trees and restoring ecosystems worldwide.

Enforce bans on illegal logging and protect indigenous lands.

Buy FSC-certified wood, reduce meat, choose palm-oil-free products.

Support NGOs, spread awareness, and join environmental movements.

Global Deforestation Hotspots

These regions face the highest deforestation rates:

  • Amazon Rainforest – Brazil, Peru, Colombia.
  • Congo Basin – Central Africa.
  • Southeast Asia – Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines.
