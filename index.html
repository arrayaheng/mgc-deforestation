This website was created to raise awareness about deforestation ‚Äî one of the most urgent environmental problems facing our planet.

Forests purify air, stabilize the climate, and support 80% of land species. Every minute, we lose over 10 million hectares of forest (FAO, 2023). This project serves as a call to action and guide to understanding how our choices impact the Earth.

üî• Causes of Deforestation

Agricultural Expansion

The leading cause of deforestation worldwide, accounting for 80% of forest loss (WWF, 2024). Land is cleared for soybeans, palm oil, and cattle ranching. This destroys habitats and contributes to carbon emissions.

Logging and Timber Industry

Logging removes millions of hectares each year for timber and paper (FAO, 2023). Unsustainable harvesting prevents forest regrowth and leads to biodiversity collapse.

Mining and Industrial Development

Mining for gold, coal, and oil pollutes rivers and soil (UNEP, 2023). The infrastructure built for extraction also clears vast forest areas.

Urbanization and Infrastructure

Cities expand into forests (World Bank, 2024). Roads and settlements fragment wildlife habitats, increasing extinction risks.

üåç Effects of Deforestation

Loss of Biodiversity

Forests house over 80% of terrestrial life (UNEP, 2023). Their destruction leads to extinction, loss of genetic diversity, and breakdowns in ecosystems that provide food, medicine, and clean air. Species such as orangutans, jaguars, and countless insects are losing their homes, which weakens entire food chains and ecosystems (WWF, 2024).

Climate Change

Forests absorb billions of tons of CO‚ÇÇ yearly (NASA, 2024). When trees are cut or burned, this carbon is released, increasing global temperatures. Deforestation disrupts rainfall patterns, intensifies droughts, and causes extreme weather like hurricanes and floods (IPCC, 2023). The loss of carbon sinks also accelerates the greenhouse effect worldwide.

Water Cycle Disruption

Trees play a vital role in maintaining the Earth's water cycle. They absorb groundwater and release moisture through transpiration, which helps form clouds and regulate rainfall. Deforested areas suffer from reduced rainfall, desertification, and water shortages, while nearby regions may face unpredictable floods (Rainforest Alliance, 2024).

Soil Erosion and Desertification

Without tree roots to stabilize the soil, heavy rain easily washes nutrients away (ScienceDirect, 2024). This decreases crop yields, damages river systems, and can turn once-fertile land into barren deserts. Soil erosion also increases sedimentation in waterways, harming aquatic habitats and water quality.

Impact on Indigenous Communities

Over 1.6 billion people rely on forests for food, shelter, and income (United Nations, 2024). Deforestation displaces Indigenous groups, erases cultural heritage, and forces migration. Losing forest access weakens traditional knowledge about medicine, agriculture, and sustainability.
