üî• Causes of Deforestation

Logging Industry

Logging for timber and paper products removes massive forest areas. Unsustainable practices lead to habitat destruction and biodiversity loss.

Large-scale commercial logging has cleared millions of hectares across Southeast Asia and the Amazon. Many of these forests never recover fully due to soil degradation and erosion. Illegal logging also contributes to 50% of tropical deforestation globally.

Agricultural Expansion

Forests are cleared for crops and livestock, especially palm oil and soy. This accounts for over 70% of tropical deforestation.

Agriculture is the single largest cause of deforestation. In Indonesia and Brazil, vast forest lands are cleared for palm oil plantations and cattle ranching, drastically affecting indigenous communities and wildlife such as orangutans and jaguars.

Urbanization

Rapid urban development requires land for housing, industries, and infrastructure.

As populations grow, forests are replaced by cities. This not only removes carbon sinks but also increases emissions from construction and energy use, creating a cycle that worsens global warming.

üíÄ Effects of Deforestation

Climate Change

Deforestation contributes to around 15% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

Trees absorb carbon dioxide. Cutting them releases carbon back into the atmosphere, accelerating climate change. The Amazon alone stores over 100 billion tons of carbon that could be released if deforestation continues.

Loss of Biodiversity

Thousands of species lose their habitats due to deforestation.

Forests house over 80% of terrestrial animals and plants. Many species, like the Sumatran tiger and Amazon river dolphin, face extinction due to habitat loss.

Soil Erosion

Tree roots anchor the soil. When forests are cleared, erosion and landslides increase.

Without tree cover, rainfall washes away nutrient-rich topsoil, leading to poor agricultural productivity and water pollution downstream.
