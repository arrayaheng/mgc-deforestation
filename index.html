üå± Purpose of This Project

This website was created to raise awareness about deforestation ‚Äî one of the most urgent environmental problems facing our planet. The goal is to educate others about what causes deforestation, its devastating effects, and the actions we can take to stop it.

Forests are essential for life: they purify air, stabilize the climate, and support more than 80% of all land-based species. Yet every minute, we lose over 10 million hectares of forest (FAO, 2023). This project serves as both a call to action and a guide to understanding how our choices impact the Earth.

üî• Causes of Deforestation

Agricultural Expansion

The leading cause of deforestation worldwide, accounting for nearly 80% of forest loss. Land is cleared for crops like soybeans, palm oil, and cattle ranching (WWF, 2024). As the global population grows, the demand for food increases, pushing agriculture deeper into tropical forests.

Logging and Timber Industry

Logging ‚Äî both legal and illegal ‚Äî removes millions of hectares of forest each year. Wood is used for paper, furniture, and construction materials. Unsustainable logging disrupts ecosystems, depletes soil nutrients, and harms endangered species habitats (FAO, 2023).

Mining and Industrial Development

Mining for gold, coal, and rare minerals devastates vast forest areas. These operations strip away vegetation, contaminate water with heavy metals, and displace Indigenous communities (UNEP, 2023; NASA, 2024).

Urbanization and Infrastructure

Expanding cities require new housing, roads, and factories, which often come at the expense of forests. Urban sprawl fragments wildlife habitats and increases human-wildlife conflicts (World Bank, 2024).

üåç Effects of Deforestation

Loss of Biodiversity

Forests are home to 80% of terrestrial species (UNEP, 2023). When these habitats disappear, species face extinction, leading to ecological imbalance and food chain disruption. The loss of biodiversity weakens the planet‚Äôs resilience to environmental changes.

Climate Change

Trees absorb carbon dioxide, one of the main greenhouse gases. When forests are cut down, that carbon is released, accelerating global warming (NASA, 2024). Deforestation accounts for nearly 10% of annual CO‚ÇÇ emissions globally.

Water Cycle Disruption

Forests regulate rainfall and prevent droughts. Deforestation disrupts this cycle, leading to irregular rainfall, flash floods, and crop failure (Rainforest Alliance, 2024).

Soil Erosion and Desertification

Without tree roots to hold it together, soil becomes loose and washes away easily. This process depletes nutrients, causes landslides, and makes land unfit for agriculture (ScienceDirect, 2024).
