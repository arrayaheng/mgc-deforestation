Introduction

Deforestation is the large-scale removal of forests, often to clear land for agriculture, logging, or urban growth. Every year, millions of hectares of forest are destroyed — an area nearly the size of England. This crisis threatens biodiversity, accelerates climate change, and harms indigenous communities.

Causes of Deforestation

  • 🌾 Agricultural expansion (soy, palm oil, cattle ranching)
  • 🪓 Logging for timber and paper
  • 🏗️ Urbanization and infrastructure projects
  • 🔥 Slash-and-burn practices
  • ⛏️ Mining and resource extraction

Effects of Deforestation

  • 🌍 Climate change (forests absorb CO₂)
  • 🐒 Loss of biodiversity — 80% of terrestrial animals live in forests
  • 💧 Water cycle disruption
  • 🏚️ Impact on indigenous peoples
  • 🌪️ Increased natural disasters (floods, landslides)

Solutions

  • 🌱 Reforestation and afforestation
  • 🛑 Stronger laws to protect rainforests
  • ♻️ Reduce, reuse, recycle paper and wood products
  • 🍫 Support sustainable agriculture (shade-grown coffee, fair-trade chocolate, palm-oil alternatives)
  • 👩‍🏫 Educating communities and spreading awareness
